Predicting Academic Performance to close the gap

Equity imbalances in higher education have meant that historically underprivileged demographic groups have been consistently underrepresented in American colleges and universities. This societal imbalance slows progress and represents underutilized economic potential. The significance of college graduates often earn more than their high school counterparts also raises questions about the future stability of California and the rest of the country.

The current population in California favors a solid majority of historically underrepresented groups. However, access to higher education continues to remain elusive for many low-income and underrepresented groups.

While 58% of Asians and 41% White young adults in California have a college degree, only 25% of African Americans and 20% of Latinos do. These trends, along with a declining population overall, mean that California is likely to face a serious skill gap in the workforce. It is projected by 2030, the demand for highly educated workers will exceed supply. A shrinking population coupled with the changing demographic will create even more pressure to meet the growing workforce demands for college graduates.
